II. Under Review

The notion of (weak) metaphysical emergence proposed by Jessica Wilson hopefully enriches our inter-level metaphysical toolkit. However, there are criticisms about Wilson’s allegedly overstated theoretical neutrality when applying her theory to the formulation of non-reductive physicalism. I start this project by arguing how a Wilsonian conception of (weak) emergence neutral with respect to the nature of properties could be difficult to defend. Later, I propose the dual-aspect model of properties—given this model, an emergent-relevant property has both a quiddity and a set of causal powers—to help friends of Wilsonian emergentism qualitatively differentiate weak emergence from strong emergence and defend the non-reduction of emergent-level properties. In addition, based on the dual aspect model of properties, a bonus—i.e., an explanation of why many emergent-level laws are ceteris paribus laws—can also be given. To summarize, I believe my proposal debugs Wilsonian (weak) emergentism.
